Pokémon Champions Counters: How to Beat the Top Meta Threats

The most-used Pokémon you'll face in Champions ranked, ordered by usage — and for each one, its weaknesses and the cleanest ways to beat it. Type matchups stack with two types (up to 4x), so use these as your starting read at team preview.

How to beat Staraptor-Mega

Fighting Flying ◆ Mega 52.9% usage

dominant Contrary attacker: Close Combat becomes a snowballing Defense/SpD boost while Brave Bird pressures redirection and Grass/Fighting cores

How to beat it
  • Electric/Ice/Flying/Psychic/Fairy x2
  • do not feed it repeated Close Combat turns; double-target early before Contrary boosts stack
  • Haze, Clear Smog, Encore/Taunt pressure, strong special Fairy/Psychic/Electric attacks and fast revenge killers are the cleanest answers
  • Ghosts dodge Close Combat but must respect Brave Bird/coverage
Why it's a threat
  • Contrary turns Close Combat drops into defensive boosts, so passive pivoting lets it snowball
  • Fighting/Flying STAB pressures Kingambit, Incineroar, Sneasler, Grass supports and many Steel cores
  • Roost sets convert defensive boosts into endgame staying power
  • pre-Mega Intimidate gives immediate physical mitigation

How to beat Garchomp

Dragon Ground 43.5% usage

Scarf revenge killer + spread Earthquake/Rock Slide

How to beat it
  • Fairy ×2 (Moonblast)
  • Ice ×4 (Icy Wind/Blizzard!)
  • Grass ×2 (Giga Drain)
  • Intimidate
  • Pelipper/Dragonite and Flying-types are immune to Earthquake
Why it's a threat
  • punishes Charizard with Rock Slide
  • punishes Steels with Earthquake
  • revenge killer with Choice Scarf

How to beat Basculegion

Water Ghost 44.5% usage

late-game sweeper/cleaner: Last Respects scales with fallen allies

How to beat it
  • priority Ghost moves (Shadow Sneak)
  • Dark ×2 (Sucker Punch/Kowtow Cleave)
  • Grass ×2 and Grassy Glide
  • Electric ×2
  • sun halves its Water moves
  • don't give it free KOs: every fallen ally charges up Last Respects
Why it's a threat
  • Last Respects = 50 + 50 for EVERY fallen ally (150 with 2 down), devastating with Adaptability
  • Aqua Jet cleans up with priority
  • Ghost: can't be trapped and immune to Fake Out
  • threatens anything that doesn't resist Water/Ghost, especially late game with teammates down
  • monstrous in rain

How to beat Kingambit

Dark Steel 42.5% usage

late-game anchor: comes in last and punishes Intimidate

How to beat it
  • Fighting ×4: Close Combat/Aura Sphere/Low Kick = death (Chople Berry blocks ONE)
  • Ground ×2
  • Fire ×2
  • Sucker Punch fails against Protect/status/switches
  • Body Press ×4 (Archaludon/Corviknight)
Why it's a threat
  • Supreme Overlord: more fallen allies = more damage
  • Defiant punishes Intimidate (+2 Atk)
  • Sucker Punch: priority against anything attacking (mind game)
  • Dark ×2 on Ghosts/Psychics and frail targets
  • immune to Psychic: walls Hatterene/Gardevoir/Delphox/Indeedee/Espathra/Oranguru

How to beat Charizard-Mega-Y

Fire Flying ◆ Mega 31.81% usage

weather engine + special spread nuke

How to beat it
  • ROCK ×2: Rock Slide everywhere is its executioner
  • Electric ×2
  • steal its weather by bringing in your own setter AFTER it
  • Thermal Exchange (Baxcalibur) gets pumped up by its Fire moves
Why it's a threat
  • instant Solar Beam in sun surprises Water-types
  • special spread nuke (Heat Wave + Weather Ball in sun)

How to beat Sinistcha

Grass Ghost 24.4% usage

redirects single-target hits with Rage Powder, heals, optional Trick Room

How to beat it
  • SPREAD moves (they ignore the redirect)
  • Fire ×2
  • Flying/Ice/Dark/Ghost ×2
  • Grass-types ignore its Rage Powder
Why it's a threat
  • Rage Powder redirects single-target hits
  • Matcha Gotcha heals
  • immune to Fake Out (it's a Ghost)

How to beat Whimsicott

Grass Fairy 20.6% usage

priority speed control + Encore/Taunt

How to beat it
  • Fire/Ice/Steel/Poison/Flying ×2 (frail, the Sash blocks 1)
  • Armor Tail blocks its priority
Why it's a threat
  • priority Tailwind (Prankster)
  • Encore: locks targets into Protect/setup
  • Taunt
  • as a Grass-type it's immune to opposing powder moves

How to beat Charizard

Fire Flying 19.18% usage

pre-Mega sun attacker; covers users who search the base preview sprite

How to beat it
  • Rock Slide ×4
  • Power Gem ×4
  • Thunderbolt ×2
  • Wave Crash ×2
  • Hydro Pump ×2
Why it's a threat

How to beat Sneasler

Fighting Poison 38.6% usage

Unburden cleaner: Close Combat + White Herb trigger the Speed double

How to beat it
  • PSYCHIC ×2: it dies
  • Flying ×2 (Brave Bird)
  • Ground ×2
  • paralysis ruins Unburden
  • Close Combat and Fake Out do 0 to Ghosts
  • extremely frail: punish it when it's left exposed
Why it's a threat
  • Close Combat drops its defenses → White Herb restores them and gets consumed → Unburden DOUBLES its Speed
  • Dire Claw: 50% poison/paralysis/sleep
  • Fake Out on top

How to beat Incineroar

Fire Dark 33.7% usage

glue support: Intimidate on every switch-in, Fake Out, Parting Shot pivot

How to beat it
  • ignore it and beat the rest of the team
  • Water/Fighting/Ground/Rock ×2
  • an Incineroar as the last mon is nearly harmless
  • Encore on the Fake Out locks it down
  • its OWN Fake Out does 0 to Ghosts
Why it's a threat
  • Intimidate on every switch-in
  • Parting Shot: debuff + pivot, BYPASSES trapping
  • wins positioning, not trades (almost zero direct damage)

How to beat Floette-Mega

Fairy ◆ Mega 23.92% usage

late-game special Calm Mind sweeper

How to beat it
  • ONLY physical attacks (Calm Mind doesn't raise physical Defense): Iron Head ×2, Sludge Bomb/Dire Claw ×2, Gigaton Hammer
  • double-target it before it stacks boosts
  • the Perish timer ignores boosts
Why it's a threat
  • after Calm Mind, SPECIAL hits bounce right off it

How to beat Sylveon

Fairy 17.9% usage

special spread nuke: Pixilate Hyper Voice ignores redirection

How to beat it
  • PHYSICAL Steel/Poison: Iron Head, Bullet Punch, Sludge, Dire Claw (low physical Defense)
  • Soundproof blocks Hyper Voice
Why it's a threat
  • Hyper Voice = Fairy spread (Pixilate) that ignores redirection (sound + spread)

How to beat Archaludon

Steel Dragon 17.43% usage

snowballing wall (Stamina) + Electro Shot cannon in rain

How to beat it
  • Fighting ×2 (Close Combat/Low Kick)
  • Ground ×2
  • WITHOUT rain Electro Shot needs a charge turn (sun cripples it)
  • kill it within 2 turns or it snowballs
Why it's a threat
  • in RAIN Electro Shot fires instantly (+1 SpA on every use)
  • with Stamina it becomes an ever-growing wall
  • Stalwart ignores redirection
  • Body Press ×4 on Kingambit/Corviknight

How to beat Farigiraf

Normal Psychic 16.12% usage

Armor Tail blocks all enemy priority; TR setter that can't be Fake Outed

How to beat it
  • Dark ×2 with NON-priority moves: Kowtow Cleave/Crunch yes, Sucker Punch no (it's priority → blocked by Armor Tail)
  • Bug ×2
  • a non-priority Taunt shuts it up
Why it's a threat
  • ARMOR TAIL blocks ALL enemy priority (Fake Out, Sucker Punch, Extreme Speed, Prankster)
  • TR setter that can't be Fake Outed
  • Thunderbolt for Water-/Flying-types
