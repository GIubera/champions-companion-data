Pokémon Champions Counters: How to Beat the Top Meta Threats

The most-used Pokémon you'll face in Champions ranked, ordered by usage — and for each one, its weaknesses and the cleanest ways to beat it. Type matchups stack with two types (up to 4x), so use these as your starting read at team preview.

How to beat Basculegion

Water Ghost 40% usage

late-game cleaner: built around Last Respects

How to beat it
  • priority Ghost moves (Shadow Sneak)
  • Dark ×2 (Sucker Punch/Kowtow Cleave)
  • Grass ×2 and Grassy Glide
  • Electric ×2
  • sun halves its Water moves
  • don't give it free KOs: every fallen ally charges up Last Respects
Why it's a threat
  • Last Respects = 50 + 50 for EVERY fallen ally (150 with 2 down), devastating with Adaptability
  • Aqua Jet cleans up with priority
  • Ghost: can't be trapped and immune to Fake Out
  • threatens anything that doesn't resist Water/Ghost, especially late game with teammates down
  • monstrous in rain

How to beat Staraptor-Mega

Fighting Flying ◆ Mega

Contrary Mega attacker: Close Combat snowballs defenses while Brave Bird pressures support pieces

How to beat it
  • Electric/Ice/Flying/Psychic/Fairy x2
  • do not feed it repeated Close Combat turns; double-target early before Contrary boosts stack
  • Haze, Clear Smog, Encore/Taunt pressure, strong special Fairy/Psychic/Electric attacks and fast revenge killers are the cleanest answers
  • Ghosts dodge Close Combat but must respect Brave Bird/coverage
Why it's a threat
  • Contrary turns Close Combat drops into defensive boosts, so passive pivoting lets it snowball
  • Fighting/Flying STAB pressures Kingambit, Incineroar, Sneasler, Grass supports and many Steel cores
  • Roost sets convert defensive boosts into endgame staying power
  • pre-Mega Intimidate gives immediate physical mitigation
